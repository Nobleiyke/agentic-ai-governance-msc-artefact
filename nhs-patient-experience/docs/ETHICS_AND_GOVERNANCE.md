# Ethics & Information Governance

Secondary analysis of **public or institutional** NHS survey data still requires a clear ethics narrative in your report.

---

## Checklist (complete in week 1–2)

- [ ] **Secondary data only** — no recruitment of patients for this project
- [ ] **Source documented** — survey name, year, wave, URL
- [ ] **Licence** — Open Government Licence or publisher terms cited
- [ ] **No re-identification** — do not publish combinations that identify individuals or rare small-area groups
- [ ] **PII scan** on any text column (names, phone, NHS numbers) before NLP
- [ ] **Secure storage** — raw microdata not on public GitHub (use `.gitignore`)
- [ ] **Module ethics** — confirm with supervisor whether formal IRB/ethics form required for desk-based secondary analysis

---

## GDPR (UK) — practical rules for students

| Principle | Project application |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Lawful basis | Public interest / research; OGL data still needs care with free text |
| Minimisation | Drop direct identifiers; analyse minimum columns needed |
| Purpose limitation | Use only for academic assessment / stated research aim |
| Storage limitation | Delete raw microdata after module ends if policy requires |

---

## What you can say in the report

**Acceptable:**

> “This study analysed anonymised aggregate FFT data published by NHS England (month X, year Y) under the Open Government Licence.”

**Avoid:**

> “Patients at Trust X said…” when quoting unless from published anonymised qualitative reports with citation.

---

## If you obtain trust-level comment extracts

Requires NHS **Information Governance** approval, Data Sharing Agreement, and secure environment — typically **beyond** a 14-week group project unless already arranged by your institution.
